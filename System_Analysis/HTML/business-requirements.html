Café & Restaurant Management Department

Business Requirements

Centralized Operational Control

business requires a unified system that manages all restaurant operations—including purchasing, inventory control, recipe costing, POS activities, table management, and billing—from a single platform.

Accurate Stock Tracking

The business needs a reliable way to track ingredient quantities in real time, detect low-stock situations, and identify discrepancies between expected and actual stock levels.

Reduction of Waste and Loss

The business requires clear visibility into consumption vs. waste to minimize losses caused by spoilage, overuse, employee mistakes, or theft.

TCost & Profit Visibility

The business must know the exact cost of each menu item based on real ingredient prices and recipes, and monitor profit margins dynamically when costs change.

Supplier & Purchasing Control

The business needs a quick, intuitive way to manage tables, track order status, assign waiters, and handle dine-in, takeaway, and delivery operations smoothly.

Fast & Accurate Billing

The business requires fast generation of bills with minimal errors, support for discounts/promotions, splitting/merging bills, and flexible payment methods.

Clear Financial Visibility

The business needs to monitor daily revenue, outstanding supplier payments, total costs, and financial performance through accurate, up-to-date reports.

Improved Staff Productivity

The system must streamline workflows for cashiers, waiters, storekeepers, and managers to reduce time wasted on manual tasks and improve service speed.

Data & Audit Trails

The business requires full history of stock movements, price changes, supplier payments, order records, and waste logs to support accountability and auditing.

Document Archiving & Traceability

The business needs to digitally store all supplier invoices, receipts, and delivery notes to simplify verification and reduce paper dependency.

Accurate Daily

    Monitoring The restaurant must be able to track:
  • items
  • sold
  • items consumed
  • revenue generated
  • stock used
  • staff
  • performance
  • discount impact on a daily basis for decision-making.

High System Reliability & Data Consistency

The business requires a stable, offline-capable desktop system (POS) that guarantees data integrity even during peak hours.

Role-Based Access Control

The business needs users to have different permissions (admin, waiter, cashier, storekeeper, accountant), ensuring sensitive operations are protected.

Scalability for Multiple Branches (Future Requirement)

The system should be designed to support multiple branches and centralized management in the future without major rework.
